An underground/independent publisher founded by John Bagley, in 1970 Company and Sons published Rory Hayes' Bogeyman Comics #3 (taking over from the San Francisco Comic Book Company), Wink Boyer's Buzzard, Boyer & Dave Geiser's Honky Tonk, and the anthology Hee Hee Comics.

The company hit pay dirt in October 1970 with Jay Kinney & Bill Griffith's Young Lust (which had been previously turned down by fellow San Francisco-based underground publishers Print Mint, Rip Off Press, and Last Gasp. The first printing of 10,000 copies sold out almost immediately, leading to more printings and more sales. Despite the first issue's success, however, Griffith and Kinney were dubious about Company & Son's accounting practices, so they brought issue #2 to Print Mint.
