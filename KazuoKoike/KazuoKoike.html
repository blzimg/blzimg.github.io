Kazuo Koike (1936–2019) was a prolific Japanese manga writer, novelist, screenwriter. He is best known for his violent, artful seinen manga, notably Lone Wolf, Lady Snowblood, and Crying Freeman, which − along with their numerous media adaptations − have been credited for their influence on the international growth of Japanese popular culture.

2004: Won Eisner Award Hall of Fame.
