Ugo Eugenio Prat, better known as Hugo Pratt (1927–1995), was an Italian comic book creator who was known for combining strong storytelling with extensive historical research on works such as Corto Maltese. In 1946 Hugo Pratt became part of the so-called Group of Venice with Fernando Carcupino, Dino Battaglia and Damiano Damiani.

In 1967, Pratt met Florenzo Ivaldi; the two created a comics magazine named after his character, Il Sergente Kirk, the hero first written by Héctor Oesterheld. In the first issue, Pratt's most famous story was published: Una ballata del mare salato (A Ballad of the Salt Sea), which introduced his best known character, Corto Maltese.

He was inducted into the Eisner Award Hall of Fame in 2005.
