Harry Conway "Bud" Fisher (1885â€“1954) was an American cartoonist who created Mutt and Jeff, the first successful daily comic strip in the United States.

He introduced A. Mutt, the comic strip that would be better known by its later title, Mutt and Jeff, on November 15, 1907, on the sports pages of the San Francisco Chronicle. During this time, newspaper cartoons appeared in a single-panel format. Fisher innovated by telling a cartoon gag in a sequence, or strip, of panels, creating the first American comic strip to successfully pioneer that since-common format.
