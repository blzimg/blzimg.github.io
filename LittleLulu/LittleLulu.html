Little Lulu is a comic strip created in 1935 by Marjorie Henderson Buell who worked under the pen name Marge. The character, Lulu Moppet, debuted in The Saturday Evening Post on February 23, 1935, in a single panel, appearing as a flower girl at a wedding and mischievously strewing the aisle with banana peels. Little Lulu replaced Carl Anderson's Henry, which had been picked up for distribution by King Features Syndicate. The Little Lulu panel continued to run weekly in The Saturday Evening Post until December 30, 1944.

Buell retained the rights, unusual for the time, and marketed Little Lulu widely throughout the 1940s.
