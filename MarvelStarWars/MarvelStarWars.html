Star Wars is a comic book series published by Marvel Comics from April 12, 1977 to May 27, 1986. The movie was released to a limited number of theaters on May 25, 1977. The first six issues adapt the film. The series chronicled their subsequent adventures for 107 issues and three annuals, including a six-issue adaptation of the 1980 sequel film The Empire Strikes Back in 1980â€“1981. In 2019, the series was revived for a single issue.

Star Wars creator George Lucas initially approached Warren Publications and DC Comics to publish a Star Wars comic book prior to the film's release as a means of publicity, then asked Roy Thomas to write the comic based on his work on Conan the Barbarian with Marvel. Stan Lee was persuaded to greenlight the project when he heard that Alec Guinness would be involved. Since movie tie-in comics rarely sold well at that time, Lee negotiated a publishing arrangement which gave no royalties to Lucasfilm until the series' sales exceeded 100,000.

The series was one of the industry's top selling titles in 1979 and 1980, with the 100,000 copy sales quota being surpassed, allowing Lippincott to renegotiate the royalty arrangements. According to former Marvel Editor-In-Chief Jim Shooter, the strong sales of Star Wars comics saved Marvel financially in 1977 and 1978.
