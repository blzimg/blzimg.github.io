Double Dragon (Daburu Doragon) is a beat 'em up video game series initially developed by Techn≈çs Japan and released as an arcade game in 1987. The series stars twin martial artists, Billy and Jimmy Lee, as they fight against various adversaries and rivals. Due to the popularity of the game series, an animated series and live-action film adaptation have also been produced. The franchise is the property of Arc System Works, the company that had ported the original Double Dragon to the Sega Master System console in 1988.

Double Dragon is an American-Canadian animated series based on the video game series of the same name. It was produced by DiC Animation City in association with Bohbot Entertainment and Tradewest, Inc., and ran for 26 half-hour episodes from 1993 to 1994.

Double Dragon is a 1994 live-action film based on the video game series of the same name and directed by James Yukich. It stars Mark Dacascos and Scott Wolf as brothers Jimmy and Billy Lee, along with Alyssa Milano as Marian Delario and Robert Patrick as antagonist Koga Shuko. The film takes place in an earthquake-crippled Los Angeles in 2007; the city is styled as a mix between a post-apocalyptic and 80s/90s punk environment.

The Comic series starts with "Enter the Double Dragon." Written by Dwayne McDuffie, pencils by Tom Raney, inks & colors by Brad Vancata, lettering by Pat Brosseau. The source of the Double Dragons' mystical powers is stolen. To get back the Dragon Statue they must face Stelth and his legions of thugs...all without their powers.
