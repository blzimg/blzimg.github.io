Sept 1940, 68 pages
Cover pencils by Bob Kane, inks by Jerry Robinson
Scripts by Bill Finger, pencils by Bob Kane, inks by Jerry Robinson and George Roussos (backgrounds).

"The Strange Case of the Diabolical Puppet Master"
The Caped Crusaders must stop the likes of the Puppet Master, who has set his sights on stealing the Voss Rifle, the Army's newly developed secret gun.

"The Ugliest Man in the World"
Batman confronts the ugliest man in the world, a man shunned by "normal" people he meets every day.

"The Crime School for Boys"
Batman has to deal with the threat of a crime school upon the lives of young boys, a school designed how to teach those boys the ways of a criminal.

"The Batman Vs. the Cat-Woman!"
The Cat returns to plague members of the Diamond Syndicate in order to achieve a beautiful "diamond prize!"

"A Blow For England" text story by Norman Goss, art by Raymond Perry. "The Batman Says" text article, Batman and Robin tell readers the negatives of crime and criminals.
