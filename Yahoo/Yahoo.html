Stories, art and cover by Joe Sacco. Long before the Internet or his award-winning Palestine graphic novel, Sacco produced this collection of stories, autobiographical and otherwise. A Crumbesque satire of capitalism, Stanton K. Pragmatron; a starving artist has an encounter with a wealthy dowager; Sacco reminisces about his low points as a person and an artist.

Sacco experiences an Almost Famous-style series of misadventures when he travels in Europe with an American rock band. Mr. Nit, who collects stamp collectors; Sacco reminisces about working at the library; a stomach-turning experience with his family. Sacco presents historical accounts from the survivors of bombing campaigns, then interviews his own grandmother Carmen, who endured Italys bombardment of Malta. Sacco wonders if hes too neurotic for a long-distance open relationship; he rages against the first Gulf War from start to finish; he struggles to understand the Palestine-Israel conflict and realizes it demands a more thorough examination. Sacco presents the crazy life of his friend Susan Catherine, a topless dancer.
