Edmond Kiraz, born Kirazian (August 25, 1923 â€“ August 11, 2020), was an Egyptian-born French-Armenian cartoonist and illustrator. Born in Cairo of Armenian descent, Kiraz began his career as a political cartoonist at 17 in Egypt, before emigrating to post-World War II Paris. In 1950 he created the comic strip Line. In 1959, while he was working for the French magazine Jours de France, his boss, Marcel Dassault, had him move from politics to humor. His cartoons are often not only humorous but slightly naughty or erotic, and since 1970 he contributed regularly to Playboy magazine.
