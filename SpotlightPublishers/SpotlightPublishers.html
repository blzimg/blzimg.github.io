Spotlight publishers only lasted from 1944 to 1945 and never released more than 1 issue per the 10 titles they had. In order to conserver space and time, I have included them all here.

Spotlight also served as a "surrogate" publisher. This means that Spotlight had a paper allotment during World War Two and published comics for others who didn't have a big enough paper allotment. Themost popular title was Jim Hardy Comics Book in 1944 by United Features Syndicate.

Single Issue Titles include:
Bee 29 the Bombardier in 1945
Gal Snaps in 1945
Gem Comics in 1945
Jeep Comics in 1945
Jolly Dolls 1945
Latest Comics in 1945
Ship Ahoy in 1944
Tailspin Comics in 1944
Three Ring Comics in 1945
Twinkle Comics in 1945
