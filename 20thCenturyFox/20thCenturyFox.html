Twentieth Century Fox Film Corporation is an American film studio that is a subsidiary of The Walt Disney Studios, a division of The Walt Disney Company. For over 83 years, it was one of the "Big Six" major American film studios formed from the merger of the Fox Film Corporation and Twentieth Century Pictures in 1935.

The big six are: Walt Disney Pictures, Warner Bros., Universal Pictures, Columbia Pictures, Paramount Pictures, and Sony Pictures. During Hollywood's golden age, other major studios were Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, RKO, and United Artists.

In 1985, the studio was acquired by News Corporation, which was succeeded by 21st Century Fox in 2013 following the spin-off of its publishing assets. In 2019, Disney acquired 20th Century Fox through its merger with 21st Century Fox.
