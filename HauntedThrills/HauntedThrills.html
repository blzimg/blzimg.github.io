None of the comics from this title have any published credits other than "Iger Shop". Farrell Publications (1940-1958) was mostly produced by the S. M. Iger Studio. In addition to packaging art for Farrell, Jerry Iger was the company's art director from 1955â€“1957.

S.M. Iger Studios started in 1937 as 'Universal Phoenix Features'. The studio went by several names. It is known as the Eisner and Iger Studio, and also as Syndicated Features Corporation.

Iger was quoted as saying, "Will had become so accomplished, and so expensive, as a free-lance artist, that the only way I could afford his services was to make him a partner". Will Eisner, in a 1997 interview, referred to the company as both "Eisner & Iger" and the "Art Syndication Company". Eisner left the firm in 1940 to produce The Spirit.

Jerry Iger is not related to comic book publisher Fred Iger. He is, however, the great-uncle of The Walt Disney Company's CEO Bob Iger. Will Eisner, meanwhile, is not related to Disney's previous CEO Michael Eisner.
