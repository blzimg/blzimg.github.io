Ace Comics was a comic book series published by David McKay Publications between 1937 and 1949, starting just before the Golden Age of Comic Books. The title reprinted syndicated newspaper strips owned by King Features Syndicate, following the successful formula of their 1936 title King Comics.

Ace Comics #11, the first appearance of The Phantom, is regarded by many to be a key issue in the history of comics, as it introduced to the comics format one of the first of the costumed heroes, leading to the Golden Age of superheroes in comics.

Even though the Phantom was a hit, it did not get onto the cover of Ace until issue #143.
