4 issues 1988 Epic (Marvel), re-released Vertigo (DC)
Illustrator: Kent Williams
Writer: J.M. DeMatteis

"Uroborous!" Close your blind eyes, what do you see? The sea turns to blood and the blood gave up its secrets!

Drums beat: there were no drums. Pipes played: there were no pipes. Something stirred inside him, an infant, a devil, crying for release.

"Theophany!" The ship glided forward. The sailors' heads lolled from side to side. Skin flaked off, drifted into the bloody sea. Gnarled arms reached out. Empty eyes demanded: Give him to us.

"Uroborous!" And they flew: Without mind, without sky, without movement. And they Flew: Hearts entwined, bodies locked, minds as one. And they flew: Above time, beyond distance, beneath space. And they flew: straight and sure to the Isle of the Dead.

DC re-releases this classic 4-issue miniseries by J.M. DeMatteis originally published by Epic (1988 Marvel), featuring new painted covers by Kent Williams.

Following the success of the Vertigo edition of J.M. DeMatteis's Moonshadow, DC re-released his eerie miniseries Blood: A Tale, featuring haunting art by Kent Williams. A mysterious man begins a passionate quest for truth which transforms him into a vampire. Descending into the Valley of Pain, he finds the first seeds of redemption in this metaphorical fever-dream, the tale of a dying king, love eternal, and a life of bloodlust.
