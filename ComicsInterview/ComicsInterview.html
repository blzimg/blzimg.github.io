David Kraft (1952) is an American comic book writer, publisher, and critic. He is primarily known for his long-running journal of interviews and criticism, Comics Interview which ran for 150 issues starting in 1983. In September 1976 Kraft became editor of FOOM with issue #15, Marvel's self-produced fan magazine, lasting as editor until the magazine's final issue (#22) in 1978. In 1974, Kraft founded the specialty science fiction publisher Fictioneer Books.

Fictioneer and its imprint Comics Interview Group published magazines including David Anthony Kraft's Comics Interview, trade journals such as Comics Revue, and the trade text 100 Hot Tips from Top Comics Creators (1994). In 1983, Kraft founded David Anthony Kraft's Comics Interview, with ran for 150 issues between 1983 and 1995, and garnered Eisner and Eagle Award nominations. As suggested by the title, each issue of Comics Interview was filled entirely with in-depth creator interviews.
